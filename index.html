Star Story 1

Situation

At the beginning of the semester, my project was focused on building a portfolio website. However, as I progressed, I realized this project didn't truly support my long-term goal of transitioning into the IT career field. Around the same time I was job hunting, I accepted a full-time job, which significantly changed my schedule and available time for coursework.

Task

I needed to reassess my project so that it aligned with my actual career goals while still fitting within the semester timeline and my new work schedule. The challenge was to refocus the project without losing momentum or falling behind.

Action

I reassessed the project purpose and documented the scope change in my Change Log. I renamed and restructured the project into the IT Department Career Advancement Project, focusing on understanding IT job requirements, identifying certifications, creating a development plan, and speaking with my manager about career pathways. I updated timelines, clarified deliverables, and communicated the revised scope to my instructor.

Result

Refocusing the project made it far more relevant to my future in IT. By the end of the semester, I had a clearer roadmap, a chosen certification, and constructive guidance from my manager. This shift strengthened my ability to manage scope changes and realign a project when goals evolve.
